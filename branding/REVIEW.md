# Brand Concept Review — RCCG Jesus House, Middletown

> **OUTCOME (2026-06-10): none of the custom concepts was adopted.** After reviewing the
> set, the user decided the site should use the **official RCCG dove seal** as its mark.
> The concepts below are kept as exploration records; the live brand kit is in
> `rccg-seal/` and documented in `BRAND.md`. The Fraunces/Mulish type pairing and the
> light/warm direction from this exploration carry forward.

> Five concepts were designed in parallel (each agent authored SVGs and visually
> self-checked its renders at 512 px and 32 px). The planned 3-judge panel died with an
> interrupted session, so this review is a single close reading of every render
> (mark-512, mark-32, lockup-800) against the three planned lenses. 2026-06-10.

All five concepts held palette discipline: navy `#1C2B4A`, red `#B3282D`, gold `#C9A227`,
cream `#FAF7EF` (red-door adds a `#8C2125` door-shadow red).

## Lens notes

**Craft & favicon legibility.** house-cross is the technical winner — three shapes, the
cross-in-house reads perfectly at 32 px. wordmark's JH stays crisp at 32 px (the door
simplifies to a red notch but the monogram identifies). red-door holds its silhouette at
32 px though panel detail dissolves. steeple-light is a charming *illustration*, not a
mark — clapboard lines and belfry detail collapse at small sizes. door-dove fails the
32 px test outright (interior becomes a blob).

**Brand fit.** red-door tells THIS parish's story best: the open door with light spilling
out is welcome-made-visible, and it rhymes with the ride ministry ("the door is open and
we'll come get you"). wordmark is warm and collegiate — right for a congregation full of
Wesleyan students. house-cross is reverent and clear but could belong to any church
anywhere. steeple-light is literal (the actual building) but illustration-y. door-dove
drifted seal-adjacent — the thing the brief warned against.

**Distinctiveness / anti-slop.** wordmark is the most ownable — passes the sweatshirt
test, looks like nothing else in the church-logo landscape. red-door's open-door portico
is distinctive. house-cross is the well-worn house+cross trope, cleanly executed.
steeple-light reads as generic church clipart. door-dove's bird figure is ambiguous
(part dove, part winged person).

## Recommendation

**Primary: red-door.** It carries the parish's actual story (arrival, welcome, the red
doors of the real building, light from inside), it's distinctive, and its lockup is the
most polished of the set (red RCCG eyebrow / serif "Jesus House" / letterspaced locale).
Its 32 px render is acceptable and can be improved with a simplified favicon variant
(drop the doorknob and panel insets at small sizes).

**Runner-up: wordmark (JH).** Strongest pure identity; keep it in reserve as a secondary
monogram (social avatars, merch, watermarks) even if red-door wins — the two share the
same palette and door motif, so they coexist naturally.

**house-cross** is the safe fallback if the pastors want maximum at-a-glance "this is a
church." **steeple-light** is retired as a logo but its building illustration could be
reused as a spot illustration on the Visit page. **door-dove** is eliminated.
